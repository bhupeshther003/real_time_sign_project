Top 10 & 25 American Sign Language Signs for Beginners

American Sign Language (ASL) is a vibrant and expressive language used by the Deaf and hard-of-hearing communities. For those just starting to learn ASL, focusing on fundamental signs can provide a solid foundation for more advanced communication. In this post, weâ€™ll cover the top 10 and 25 essential ASL signs that every beginner should learn first.

Top 10 ASL Signs for Beginners

  • Hello - A simple and friendly greeting. Wave your hand outward from your forehead.
  • Thank You - To show gratitude, extend your fingers from your chin and move your hand outward.
  • Please - Rub your open hand in a circular motion over your chest.
  • Sorry - Make a fist and rub it in a circular motion over your chest.
  • Yes - Nod your fist up and down, resembling a head nod.
  • No - Pinch your thumb and fingers together, mimicking a mouth saying "no."
  • Help - Place one hand in a fist, and use the other hand to lift it up from the fist's base.
  • More - Touch the tips of your fingers together with both hands.
  • Eat - Use the tips of your fingers to mimic putting food into your mouth.
  • Drink - Cup your hand as if holding a glass and bring it to your mouth.

Top 25 ASL Signs for Beginners

  • Goodbye - Wave your hand away from your face, similar to a casual farewell.
  • Friend - Interlock your index fingers and then pull them apart while keeping them close.
  • Please - Same as mentioned before; it's important to use this sign to show politeness.
  • Name - Tap your fingers together twice in front of your chin, resembling a name tag.
  • Love - Cross your arms over your chest in an "X" shape to signify affection.
  • Family - Use both hands to form an "F" shape and then move them outward in a circular motion.
  • Happy - Place your hands on your chest and move them outward in a slight curve.
  • Sad - Use both hands to drag your fingers down your cheeks, mimicking tears.
  • Help - Same as mentioned before; a versatile and crucial sign.
  • Bathroom - Tap your thumb on your fingers in a fist, which is the sign for "bathroom."
  • Dog - Pat your thigh with your hand, mimicking calling a dog.
  • Cat - Use your fingers to mimic whiskers on your face.
  • House - Outline the shape of a house by making a roof with your hands and then framing it.
  • School - Tap the fingertips of both hands together, resembling a bell or classroom gesture.
  • Book - Open your hands as if opening a book, then close them together.
